Urban Spatial Analytics

Academic Research Showcase

Street View Data for
Urban Crime Pattern Analysis

Crime statistics tell us where crime happens. Street-level imagery reveals why. This research quantifies how the physical character of urban space — visibility, land use, vegetation, and built form — shapes crime risk across 3,525 London neighbourhoods.

Methodology & Data Pipeline

The end-to-end framework translating raw imagery into statistical insights.

Data Pipeline Workflow

Our pipeline downloads street view photos, performs spatial overlays onto London neighborhood boundaries, extracts semantic image classes (vegetation, roads, buildings) using SegFormer-B0, and computes semantic urban labels with UrbanCLIP.

Step 01

Data Extraction & Preprocessing

Generated a regular 50m x 50m grid across Greater London (629,375 centers). Fetched 150,654 optimal street-level images via Mapillary API.

Step 02

Spatial Joining & LSOA Alignment

Mapped coordinates to London Lower Layer Super Output Areas (LSOAs), matching 150,182 visual samples across boundaries with GIS centroids.

Step 03

Feature Standardization

Extracted 19 environmental features using SegFormer-B0 (Cityscapes) and probabilistic urban function classes using UrbanCLIP (ViT-B-32).

Step 04

Statistical Inference

Aggregated features to LSOA level (70.7% data coverage) and executed Pearson/Spearman correlation and Kruskal-Wallis H tests.

Step 05

Crime Data Integration

Merged street features with Metropolitan Police Service open records (Jan 2019 – Jun 2025), covering 10 major crime categories across Greater London LSOAs.
